Dr. Julio Maiztegui

The creator of hemorrhagic fever vaccine Argentine

Dr. Julio Maiztegui (seated below, on the left) with his work team.

Is amaizing the work of Dr. Julio I. Maiztegui regarding Argentine hemorrhagic fever (FHA), a disease that has only been found in part of Argentina. Maiztegui dedicated himself to the fight against this disease, carrying out health education campaigns for prophylaxis, researching the possible treatment, and collaborating with other scientific teams to unveil its etiology and develop a vaccine. The National Institute of Human Viral Diseases, which was created by him and bears his name, is responsible for maintaining in Argentina the now successful prophylaxis and for the eventual treatment of the disease, that was once a serious health concern.

  • 1931- The Dr. Maiztegui was born in August 25 in the city Bahia Blanca, Buenos Aires province of Argentina.
  • 1957- He graduated as a doctor at the University of Buenos Aires.
  • 1964- He earned a Master's in Public Health from Harvard University.
  • 1965- He returns to Argentina to work at the CEMIC (Center for Medical Education and Clinical Research) and with the support of the Emilio Ocampo Foundation, he begins his work at Pergamino
  • 1968-1970- He traveled to London to obtain a Master's degree in Epidemiology, at the University of London School of Tropical Medicine.
  • 1971- Within his research on Argentine hemorrhagic fever, he managed to demonstrate that the 30% mortality was reduced to only 3% if patients were treated with plasma from people who were already sick before the eighth day after contracting the disease.
  • 1978- He began directing the National Institute for Studies on Hemorrhagic Viruses (INEVH), created that same year to continue research, an institute of which he was director until his death.
  • 1993- At the age of 62, he died in the city of Pergamino due to cancer.
